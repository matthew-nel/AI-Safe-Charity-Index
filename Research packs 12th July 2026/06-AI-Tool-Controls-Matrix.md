# AI Tool Controls Matrix

## Executive summary

This pack compares key controls for major workplace AI tools likely to be used by UK charities: ChatGPT Business/Enterprise (OpenAI), Claude Team/Enterprise (Anthropic), Microsoft 365 Copilot, Google Workspace with Gemini, Notion AI, Perplexity Enterprise, Canva AI and Adobe Firefly.[web:143][web:175][web:148][web:158][web:160][web:193] It focuses on training on customer data, retention, admin controls, connectors and data access, deletion, residency, encryption, SSO/SCIM, data-processing terms, subprocessors, compliance claims, human review, copyright/IP positions, and nonprofit pricing or discounts.

Where vendors do not publish specific information (for example, detailed default retention for all content types or precise residency guarantees for every tier), this pack records **“not confirmed in public documentation as of 2026-07-13”** rather than inferring features.

## Scope

The matrix covers:

- ChatGPT Business and ChatGPT Enterprise.
- Claude Team and Claude Enterprise.
- Microsoft 365 Copilot for business (including Copilot Chat).[web:175][web:129]
- Google Workspace with Gemini (Business and Enterprise editions).[web:148][web:154][web:157]
- Notion AI (including Enterprise).[web:158][web:164][web:172]
- Perplexity Enterprise Pro.[web:160][web:168][web:188]
- Canva (AI features) and Adobe Firefly, from trust/privacy documentation.[web:189][web:193]

## Method

Sources are primarily official vendor documentation, trust centres, help articles and DPA summaries, supplemented by neutral professional analyses where helpful.[web:143][web:175][web:118][web:148][web:158][web:160][web:193] Only documented features are included; marketing claims about effectiveness or “safety” are not treated as independent evidence.

## Bonus: charity-friendly pricing and access

Many of these tools offer charity or nonprofit discounts, often via intermediaries rather than direct public lists:

- Microsoft 365 (and thus Copilot) and Google Workspace both have longstanding nonprofit licensing programmes that reduce core licence costs; Copilot and Gemini add-ons may be available at discounted rates but require direct enquiry.[web:173][web:154]
- Canva for Nonprofits provides free Canva Pro access for eligible charities, which includes AI features; however, organisations should still avoid using AI for sensitive beneficiary content.[web:189]
- Other vendors (OpenAI, Anthropic, Notion, Perplexity, Adobe) reference startup, education or enterprise discount models; explicit UK-charity pricing is rarely published and should be treated as **“not confirmed in public documentation as of 2026-07-13”** until verified with sales.[web:143][web:172][web:170]

## Practical implications for UK charities

- Consumer vs enterprise divide: For all tools, consumer/free accounts typically allow training on user data and offer weaker controls; charities should prioritise organisational accounts with clear DPAs and training-off defaults.[web:133][web:115][web:186][web:126]
- Connector risk: For tools that connect to SharePoint, OneDrive, Google Drive, Slack or CRMs, effective safety depends on existing permission hygiene; oversharing often arises from misconfigured source systems rather than the AI layer.[web:128][web:136][web:148][web:159]
- Charity size: Micro and small charities may favour tools with simpler admin models (e.g. Notion AI with training off by default, Perplexity Enterprise where feasible); larger charities can exploit extended audit, DLP and retention controls in Microsoft and Google ecosystems.[web:164][web:160][web:128][web:154]
