# Purpose

This document defines how the knowledge base should support charity trustees, boards and senior leadership teams in governing the safe, responsible and proportionate use of artificial intelligence.

It translates regulator guidance into practical governance actions suitable for UK charities.

It is not legal advice.

---

# Core Principle

> Trustees are accountable for how AI is used, even when they do not use AI themselves.

AI governance should strengthen a charity's mission, protect beneficiaries and maintain public trust.

---

# Objectives

Help trustees:

- understand opportunities and risks
- ask the right questions
- approve proportionate AI use
- oversee governance
- support staff confidence
- protect reputation and trust

---

# Trustee Responsibilities

Trustees should ensure the charity has:

- an AI acceptable use policy
- clear ownership of AI
- appropriate staff training
- approved AI tools
- proportionate data protection controls
- safeguarding oversight
- incident reporting
- regular policy reviews

---

# Questions Trustees Should Ask

## Strategy

- Why are we using AI?
- Which organisational problems are we solving?
- Does AI support our charitable purpose?

## Risk

- What could go wrong?
- What data is being used?
- Who could be harmed?
- What is our biggest AI risk?

## Governance

- Who owns AI internally?
- How are new tools approved?
- How often are policies reviewed?
- How do staff report concerns?

## Data Protection

- Are staff using public AI tools?
- Are personal or special category data involved?
- When are DPIAs required?
- Are approved tools available?

## Safeguarding

- Could AI affect vulnerable people?
- Is meaningful human oversight in place?
- Are safeguarding leads involved where needed?

---

# Board Approval Checklist

Before approving AI use, trustees should confirm:

- Purpose is clear
- Benefits outweigh risks
- Governance exists
- Human oversight exists
- Staff are trained
- AI policy is approved
- Data protection considered
- Safeguarding considered
- Review dates agreed

---

# AI Risk Appetite

Encourage charities to define:

## Low Risk

Examples:

- drafting public content
- meeting agendas
- brainstorming

## Medium Risk

Examples:

- anonymised internal analysis
- internal planning
- workflow automation

## High Risk

Examples:

- safeguarding
- HR casework
- eligibility decisions
- personal data at scale
- automated decisions affecting people

---

# What Trustees Should Not Do

Trustees should not:

- assume AI decisions are objective
- rely on AI without human review
- ignore staff concerns
- approve unknown tools without checks
- treat AI policy as a one-off exercise

---

# Governance Documents

Recommended documents:

- AI Use Policy
- Approved Tools Register
- AI Risk Register
- Incident Log
- Training Record
- Review Schedule
- DPIA Register where appropriate

---

# Quarterly Board Review

Suggested standing agenda:

1. AI updates
2. New tools
3. Incidents
4. Research findings
5. Policy changes
6. Regulatory updates
7. Training completion
8. Next priorities

---

# Trustee Dashboard

Useful metrics:

- approved AI tools
- policy review status
- staff trained
- AI incidents
- open risks
- high-risk projects
- monthly research completed
- quarterly assurance completed
