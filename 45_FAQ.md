# AI Use

## Can charities use AI?

Yes.

AI can help with drafting, summarising, planning, editing, research support and routine tasks.

The main question is not whether charities can use AI, but whether the specific use is appropriate and safe.

## What is a safe first use?

Start with:

- public information
- fictional examples
- brainstorming
- grammar
- formatting
- plain-English rewriting

Avoid personal, confidential or safeguarding information.

## Is AI always accurate?

No.

AI can produce incorrect, incomplete or invented information.

Always review important outputs.

## Can AI replace professional judgement?

No.

AI should not replace:

- safeguarding decisions
- legal advice
- trustee accountability
- employment decisions
- clinical judgement
- significant decisions about people

# Before You Paste

## What should I never paste into a public AI tool?

Do not paste:

- safeguarding records
- identifiable beneficiary information
- donor databases
- payroll
- HR case files
- medical information
- passwords
- API keys
- privileged legal advice
- confidential contracts

## What if I remove someone's name?

Removing a name may not be enough.

A person can still be identified from:

- job title
- location
- age
- health condition
- family circumstances
- unusual events

## Can I use anonymised information?

Sometimes.

The information must be genuinely anonymised, not simply missing a name.

If someone could still be identified, treat it as personal data.

## What does Green, Amber and Red mean?

- **Green:** Go ahead with normal checks.
- **Amber:** Pause and check.
- **Red:** Stop.

The rating applies to the use case, not the tool brand alone.

# GDPR and Data Protection

## Does GDPR apply to AI?

Yes, when personal data is involved.

AI does not remove data-protection responsibilities.

## What is personal data?

Personal data is information about an identified or identifiable person.

Examples include:

- names
- email addresses
- phone numbers
- donor histories
- volunteer records
- case notes

## What is special category data?

This includes information about:

- health
- ethnicity
- religion
- political opinions
- trade union membership
- sexual orientation
- genetics
- biometrics

This data requires stronger protection.

## Do we need a DPIA?

Possibly, if the AI use is likely to create a high risk to people's rights and freedoms.

Examples may include:

- profiling
- automated decisions
- vulnerable people
- large-scale sensitive data
- eligibility decisions

## Can we use personal data if the tool is approved?

Approval alone is not enough.

You still need:

- a lawful basis
- a clear purpose
- data minimisation
- appropriate security
- transparency
- human oversight

# Safeguarding

## Can AI be used in safeguarding work?

Only with extreme caution.

AI may help with generic training or policy drafting.

It should not be used to:

- assess immediate risk
- decide whether abuse occurred
- replace safeguarding leads
- process real case details casually
- make referral decisions

## Can I paste a safeguarding disclosure into ChatGPT?

No.

Do not paste real safeguarding disclosures into a general-purpose AI tool.

Use your organisation's safeguarding process.

## Does anonymisation make safeguarding data safe?

Not automatically.

Safeguarding information may remain highly sensitive even after names are removed.

# AI Policies and Governance

## Does every charity need an AI policy?

Most charities using AI should have at least simple, written rules.

A small charity may only need:

- permitted uses
- prohibited uses
- approved tools
- who to ask
- how to report an issue
- review date

## Who should own AI in a charity?

Someone should have clear responsibility.

This may be:

- a manager
- digital lead
- data-protection lead
- senior leader
- trustee

The exact role depends on charity size.

## Do trustees need to know about AI?

Yes.

Trustees do not need to become technical experts, but they should understand:

- current use
- major risks
- policies
- incidents
- high-risk projects
- review arrangements

## Should we keep an AI-use register?

It is a sensible step when AI use is regular or spread across teams.

A register can record:

- tool
- task
- data used
- owner
- risk level
- approval
- review date

# AI Tools

## Is ChatGPT safe for charities?

It depends on:

- the account
- the plan
- the task
- the data
- organisational controls
- human review

No tool should be described as simply safe or unsafe.

## Is Claude safer than ChatGPT?

There is no simple universal answer.

Compare:

- plan
- account type
- privacy controls
- retention
- training-data statements
- admin controls
- intended use

## Is Microsoft Copilot automatically safe because it is in Microsoft 365?

No.

Microsoft 365 permissions, licences and tenant settings still matter.

Poor access controls can create wider risk.

## Is Gemini safe inside Google Workspace?

It may offer stronger organisational controls than a personal account, but the specific product, plan and settings must be checked.

## Is a paid account always safer than a free account?

Not always.

Paid or business plans may offer stronger controls, but you still need to review:

- data handling
- admin controls
- retention
- permissions
- contractual terms

## Can volunteers use their own AI accounts?

Usually not with charity data.

Volunteers should follow the same rules as staff and use approved accounts where required.

# Everyday Charity Work

## Can AI help write a funding bid?

Yes, for:

- structure
- first drafts
- plain English
- idea development

Do not upload donor data, confidential funder information or identifiable case studies without approval.

## Can AI write social media posts?

Yes.

Use public information and review:

- facts
- tone
- accessibility
- representation
- safeguarding
- copyright

## Can AI summarise meeting notes?

Sometimes.

Pause if the meeting involved:

- safeguarding
- HR
- health
- legal advice
- confidential trustee matters

## Can AI analyse survey responses?

Yes, if the data is suitably anonymised or aggregated and the tool is approved.

Do not upload raw identifiable responses casually.

## Can AI help with recruitment?

It can help draft:

- job descriptions
- interview questions
- training material

Do not use it to make final recruitment decisions or rank candidates without appropriate governance.

## Can AI draft policies?

Yes, as a first draft.

Policies must still be reviewed against:

- current law
- regulator guidance
- charity practice
- organisational context

# Human Review

## What should I check before using AI output?

Check:

- factual accuracy
- source support
- tone
- bias
- accessibility
- legal meaning
- confidentiality
- charity relevance

## Who is responsible if AI gets it wrong?

The charity and the people using the output remain responsible.

Responsibility does not transfer to the AI tool.

## Can we publish AI-generated content without editing?

That is not recommended.

Human review is essential.

# Research and Feedback

## How can my charity contribute?

You can:

- report an error
- suggest a topic
- share a workflow
- join research
- submit feedback
- open a GitHub issue
- propose a contribution

## Can we share a real case study?

Only if:

- you have permission
- privacy is protected
- safeguarding is considered
- sensitive details are removed
- publication is appropriate

Anonymised or fictionalised examples are usually safer.

## How is research data used?

Research findings should be published in aggregated or anonymised form.

Raw personal data should not be placed in the public repository.
