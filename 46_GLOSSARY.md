# Glossary

This glossary explains common AI, data protection, safeguarding, governance and digital terms in plain English.

The definitions are written for charity staff, trustees and volunteers who may not be technical specialists.

Where a legal term matters, use the full legal or regulatory guidance alongside this glossary.

---

# A

## Access control

Rules and settings that decide who can view, edit or use information or systems.

## Accountability

Being able to show who is responsible for a decision, what checks were completed and why an action was taken.

## AI

Artificial intelligence.

A broad term for systems that perform tasks commonly associated with human intelligence, such as writing, recognising patterns, summarising or making predictions.

## AI assistant

A tool that helps with tasks such as drafting, editing, summarising, planning or research support.

Examples include ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini and Copilot.

## AI hallucination

When an AI system produces information that sounds convincing but is false, invented or unsupported.

## AI policy

A document explaining how an organisation allows, restricts and governs AI use.

## AI system inventory

A list of AI tools, integrations and automated systems used by an organisation.

## AI-use register

A record of how AI is being used, by whom, for what task and with what data.

## Algorithm

A set of instructions used by a computer to process information or complete a task.

## Algorithmic bias

Unfair or distorted outcomes caused by data, design choices or system behaviour.

## Amber

A traffic-light rating meaning:

> Pause and check.

Amber uses may require approval, anonymisation, an approved tool, stronger controls or specialist review.

## Anonymisation

Changing information so that no individual can reasonably be identified.

Removing a name alone may not be enough.

## API

Application Programming Interface.

A technical way for one system to exchange data or instructions with another.

## API key

A secret code used to authenticate access to an online service.

API keys should never be pasted into public AI tools or committed to GitHub.

## Approved tool

An AI tool that an organisation has reviewed and allowed for defined uses.

Approval does not mean every use is safe.

## Automated decision-making

A decision made by a system without meaningful human involvement.

Some automated decisions may create additional legal rights or risks.

---

# B

## Beneficiary

A person or group intended to benefit from a charity's work.

Some charities prefer terms such as service user, participant, member or community member.

## Bias

A systematic tendency that may lead to unfair, inaccurate or unbalanced outputs.

## Biometrics

Physical or behavioural characteristics used to identify someone, such as facial recognition or fingerprints.

Some biometric data is special category data.

## Board

The group of trustees responsible for governing a charity.

---

# C

## Chatbot

A system designed to interact with people through written or spoken conversation.

## Claim Register

A record linking important public claims to supporting evidence and the pages where those claims appear.

## Claude Projects

A Claude workspace feature used to keep project instructions, documents and recurring context together.

## Cloud service

A digital service hosted and operated online by an external provider.

## Confidential information

Information that should not be shared widely.

Examples include internal contracts, board disputes, legal advice or unpublished financial information.

## Consent

A freely given, specific, informed and unambiguous agreement.

Consent is one possible lawful basis under UK GDPR, but it is not always the most appropriate one.

## Controller

The organisation that decides why and how personal data is used.

## Copilot

A family of AI tools associated with Microsoft products and services.

## Copyright

Legal protection for original creative work.

AI output and training may create copyright questions, especially where source material is copied or transformed.

---

# D

## Data breach

A security incident that leads to personal data being lost, accessed, changed, disclosed or destroyed without proper authorisation.

## Data minimisation

Using only the personal data genuinely needed for the task.

## Data Processing Agreement

A contract that explains how a supplier processes personal data on behalf of an organisation.

## Data Protection Impact Assessment

A structured assessment of privacy risks.

A DPIA may be required where processing is likely to create a high risk to people's rights and freedoms.

## Data Protection Officer

A formal role required in some organisations under data-protection law.

Many small charities will not be legally required to appoint one.

## Data retention

How long information is stored.

## Dataset

A structured collection of information used for analysis, training or testing.

## Decision tree

A step-by-step set of questions that leads to a decision or recommendation.

## Deepfake

Synthetic audio, video or imagery designed to make it appear that a real person said or did something they did not.

## Digital maturity

How capable and confident an organisation is in using digital tools, systems, data and governance.

## DPIA

Data Protection Impact Assessment.

---

# E

## Encryption

A security method that makes information unreadable without the correct key.

## Enterprise account

A plan designed for larger organisations, usually with stronger administration, contracts and security controls.

Enterprise features vary by provider.

## Evidence level

A rating that shows how strong, current and authoritative the evidence is.

## Explainability

The ability to understand and explain how an AI-supported decision or output was produced.

---

# F

## Fine-tuning

Adapting an AI model using additional training data for a particular task or style.

## Foundation model

A large general-purpose AI model that can be adapted to many tasks.

## Frontmatter

Structured metadata placed at the top of a Markdown file.

It can include title, version, status, review date and source IDs.

---

# G

## Gemini

Google's family of AI models and assistant products.

## Generative AI

AI that creates new content such as text, images, audio, video or code.

## Git

A version-control system used to track changes to files.

## GitHub

A platform for hosting and managing Git repositories.

It supports issues, pull requests, releases and collaboration.

## Governance

The rules, responsibilities and controls used to oversee decisions and manage risk.

## Green

A traffic-light rating meaning:

> Go ahead with normal checks.

Green uses still require factual and human review.

## Grounding

Giving an AI system reliable source material or context to reduce unsupported answers.

Grounding does not guarantee accuracy.

---

# H

## Hallucination

See AI hallucination.

## High-risk use

An AI use that could significantly affect people's rights, safety, employment, services or access to support.

## Human in the loop

A person meaningfully reviews or controls an AI-supported process.

## Human oversight

Active human responsibility for reviewing, challenging and approving important AI outputs or decisions.

---

# I

## Identifiable person

A person who can be recognised directly or indirectly from the available information.

## Incident log

A record of mistakes, near misses, breaches or other problems connected to AI use.

## Inference

A conclusion or prediction produced from available information.

AI systems may infer sensitive characteristics even when they were not directly provided.

## Information architecture

The way content, pages and navigation are organised.

## International transfer

Sending or making personal data available outside the UK.

## ISO

International Organization for Standardization.

ISO publishes standards used in areas such as security, quality and AI governance.

---

# J

## Jurisdiction

The country or legal area whose rules apply.

A source from another country may not reflect UK law.

---

# K

## Knowledge base

A structured collection of information designed to help people find answers and guidance.

## Knowledge management

The practice of organising, maintaining and sharing information so it remains useful.

---

# L

## Large language model

An AI model trained on large amounts of text and designed to work with language.

## Lawful basis

A valid legal reason for processing personal data under UK GDPR.

## Learning path

A structured route through content for a particular role, level or goal.

## Legal privilege

Protection that can apply to confidential communications between a client and legal adviser.

## LLM

Large language model.

---

# M

## Machine learning

A type of AI where systems identify patterns from data rather than following only fixed instructions.

## Markdown

A simple text format used to create structured documents and web pages.

## Metadata

Information about a page or file, such as title, version, review date or audience.

## Model

The underlying AI system that processes prompts and produces outputs.

## Model training

The process of using data to develop or improve an AI model.

---

# N

## NCSC

National Cyber Security Centre.

A UK public body providing cybersecurity guidance.

## NIST

National Institute of Standards and Technology.

A US public body that publishes standards and risk frameworks, including AI guidance.

## Notion AI

AI features available within the Notion workspace platform.

---

# O

## Open source

Software or content made available under a licence that permits defined forms of access, reuse or contribution.

## Output

The response, result or content produced by an AI tool.

## Output review

The process of checking AI-generated content for accuracy, bias, safety, tone and suitability.

---

# P

## Personal account

An individual account controlled by the user rather than by their organisation.

Personal accounts may not provide appropriate organisational controls.

## Personal data

Information about an identified or identifiable person.

## Plain English

Clear writing designed to be understood without specialist knowledge.

## Privacy notice

A document explaining how personal data is collected, used, stored and shared.

## Processor

An organisation that processes personal data on behalf of a controller.

## Profiling

Using personal data to evaluate or predict aspects of a person, such as behaviour, interests, performance or risk.

## Prompt

The instruction or information entered into an AI system.

## Pseudonymisation

Replacing direct identifiers with codes or substitutes.

Pseudonymised data is still personal data if it can be linked back to a person.

## Pull request

A GitHub workflow used to propose, review and merge changes.

---

# Q

## Quarterly assurance

A deeper scheduled review of the knowledge base, evidence, accessibility, navigation and governance.

---

# R

## Red

A traffic-light rating meaning:

> Stop.

Red uses should not proceed through a general-purpose AI tool without formal approval and suitable safeguards.

## Release

A published version of the knowledge base.

## Release notes

A summary of what was added, changed, corrected, removed or deprecated in a release.

## Repository

A version-controlled collection of files, history and project configuration.

## Responsible AI

An approach to designing or using AI that considers fairness, safety, accountability, transparency and human impact.

## Review date

The date when guidance was last checked or is due to be checked again.

## Risk register

A structured record of risks, likelihood, impact, controls and ownership.

---

# S

## Safeguarding

The responsibility to protect children and adults at risk from abuse, neglect or harm.

## Sensitive information

Information that could create harm, distress or unfair treatment if misused or exposed.

## Shadow AI

AI use that happens without organisational awareness, approval or governance.

## Source ID

A unique identifier assigned to a source in the Source Register.

## Source Register

A record of evidence used by the project.

## Special category data

Personal data requiring additional protection under UK GDPR.

Examples include health, ethnicity, religion, political opinions, trade union membership and sexual orientation.

## Static website

A website generated from files in advance rather than assembled dynamically for each visitor.

## Synthetic data

Artificially created data designed to resemble real data without directly representing real people.

Synthetic data may still create risk if it reproduces identifiable patterns.

---

# T

## Terms of service

Rules governing the use of a product or platform.

## Tool approval

The process of checking and authorising an AI tool for defined organisational uses.

## Training data

Information used to train or improve an AI model.

## Transparency

Explaining clearly how AI is used, what data is involved and who remains responsible.

## Trustee

A person with legal responsibility for governing a charity.

---

# U

## UK GDPR

The United Kingdom General Data Protection Regulation.

## User research

Research that helps understand people's needs, behaviour, questions and experience.

---

# V

## Vendor

A company or organisation supplying a product or service.

## Vendor lock-in

A situation where it becomes difficult or expensive to move away from a particular provider.

## Version control

A system for recording changes to files over time.

## VitePress

A static documentation-site framework built around Markdown.

## Vulnerable person

A broad, context-dependent term.

The project should avoid defining people only by vulnerability and should use precise language where possible.

---

# W

## WCAG

Web Content Accessibility Guidelines.

A recognised standard for making digital content accessible.

## Workflow

A sequence of steps used to complete a task.

## Workspace

A shared digital environment used by a team or organisation.

---

# Z

## Zero-result search

A website search that returns no useful result.

Tracking these searches can reveal missing guidance or unclear navigation.

---

# Terms to Avoid or Use Carefully

The following terms may be unclear or misleading if used without explanation:

- AI-powered
- intelligent
- safe
- secure
- anonymous
- objective
- human-like
- unbiased
- compliant
- enterprise-grade
- transformative

Do not use absolute claims unless they are supported and properly qualified.

---

# Glossary Maintenance

Add or update a term when:

- readers repeatedly ask what it means
- a new technical concept enters the guidance
- a regulator introduces new terminology
- a provider changes product language
- a term is being used inconsistently
- user research identifies confusion

Each definition should:

- use plain English
- remain accurate
- avoid circular definitions
- include a charity-relevant explanation where useful
- link to deeper guidance where available

---

# Success Criteria

The glossary succeeds when:

- non-technical readers can understand the knowledge base
- technical and legal terms are used consistently
- readers do not need to leave the site for basic definitions
- definitions remain accurate and proportionate
- jargon does not become a barrier to safe AI use
