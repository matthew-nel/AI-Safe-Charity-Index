# Governance

This document explains how AI Safe Charity Index is governed.

Governance exists to ensure the project remains evidence-led, transparent, independent, accountable, practical and sustainable.

## Current Governance Model

At launch:

- Original Creator: Matthew James Nel
- Primary Maintainer: Matthew James Nel

The maintainer is responsible for releases, editorial decisions, evidence standards, contributor approval, roadmap direction and urgent corrections.

## Governance Principles

Governance decisions should be guided by:

- people before technology
- evidence before opinion
- transparency
- proportionality
- accessibility
- accountability
- continuous improvement

## Risk-Based Review

Higher-risk topics require additional care. Examples include safeguarding, GDPR, employment, legal interpretation, automated decision-making and vulnerable people.

These topics may require specialist review before publication.

## Editorial Independence

Editorial decisions must not be influenced by sponsorship, affiliate income, commercial partnerships, vendor requests or advertising.

Any financial relationship should be disclosed publicly.

## Release Governance

Every release should include a version number, release notes, changelog entry, reviewed evidence, updated review dates and contributor acknowledgements where appropriate.
