# Purpose

This document explains how the AI Safe Use Knowledge Base is governed.

Governance exists to ensure the project remains:

- evidence-led
- transparent
- independent
- accountable
- practical
- sustainable

The governance model should evolve as the project grows without compromising its core principles.

---

# Governance Objectives

The project aims to:

- protect editorial independence
- maintain evidence quality
- manage contributions fairly
- review high-risk guidance carefully
- publish changes transparently
- protect privacy and safeguarding
- support long-term sustainability

---

# Governance Principles

All governance decisions should be guided by:

- people before technology
- evidence before opinion
- transparency
- proportionality
- accessibility
- accountability
- continuous improvement

- privacy

---

# Editorial Independence

Editorial decisions must not be influenced by:

- sponsorship
- affiliate income
- commercial partnerships
- vendor requests
- advertising

Any financial relationship should be disclosed publicly.

---

# Evidence Governance

Material guidance should:

- reference reliable sources
- link to the Source Register
- link to the Claim Register
- record review dates
- document limitations

Where evidence is uncertain, the uncertainty should be explained.

---

# Risk-Based Review

Higher-risk topics require additional care.

Examples:

- safeguarding
- GDPR
- employment
- legal interpretation
- automated decision-making
- vulnerable people

These topics may require specialist review before publication.

---

# Release Governance

Every release should include:

- version number
- release notes
- changelog
- reviewed evidence
- updated review dates
- contributor acknowledgements where appropriate

Emergency corrections should be documented.

---

# Conflict of Interest

Contributors should disclose:

- employment by AI providers
- consultancy work
- sponsorship
- affiliate relationships
- commercial interests

Disclosure supports transparency rather than preventing participation.

---

# Research Governance

Research should be:

- proportionate
- privacy conscious
- voluntary
- transparent
- appropriately anonymised

Research findings published by the project should explain:

- methodology
- limitations
- sample
- date
- interpretation

---

# Privacy

The project should minimise personal data collection.

Public repositories must never contain:

- safeguarding records
- identifiable beneficiary data
- employee records
- confidential organisational information
- raw research responses

---

# Accessibility Governance

Accessibility should be considered during:

- design
- writing
- development
- review
- release
- maintenance

Accessibility issues should be prioritised alongside factual corrections.

---

# Governance Review

Review this document:

- quarterly
- after major governance changes
- after significant incidents
- before major public releases

Record material changes in the changelog.

---

# Long-Term Vision

The governance model should remain lightweight enough for an independent project while being robust enough to earn the trust of charities, contributors and future partners.

---

# Success Criteria

Governance succeeds when:

- decisions are transparent
- evidence is trustworthy
- contributors understand responsibilities
- risks are managed proportionately
- editorial independence is maintained
- charities can rely on the guidance with confidence
