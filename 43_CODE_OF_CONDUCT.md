# Code of Conduct

The AI Safe Use Knowledge Base for UK Charities is built around trust, practical learning and respectful collaboration.

Everyone who participates in the project should feel able to contribute without being dismissed, intimidated or exposed to unnecessary harm.

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- GitHub issues
- pull requests
- discussions
- research participation
- surveys
- interviews
- workshops
- project email
- feedback forms
- public events
- social media conversations connected to the project
- contributor spaces
- project documentation

---

# Our Commitment

We are committed to creating a respectful, inclusive and accessible environment for:

- charity staff
- trustees
- volunteers
- researchers
- people with lived experience
- technical contributors
- safeguarding specialists
- data-protection professionals
- people new to AI
- people with different levels of digital confidence

Participation should not depend on technical expertise.

---

# Expected Behaviour

Contributors and participants should:

- communicate respectfully
- assume good faith where reasonable
- give constructive feedback
- explain technical terms
- respect different levels of confidence
- protect privacy
- respect lived experience
- acknowledge uncertainty
- cite evidence where possible
- correct mistakes openly
- avoid personal attacks
- focus criticism on ideas and work
- make space for others
- follow safeguarding and data-protection rules

---

# Unacceptable Behaviour

Unacceptable behaviour includes:

- harassment
- discrimination
- threats
- intimidation
- personal attacks
- deliberate humiliation
- sexual harassment
- stalking
- doxxing
- publishing private information
- sharing safeguarding information
- sharing identifiable beneficiary information
- sharing confidential charity information
- using aggressive or demeaning language
- repeated bad-faith disruption
- deliberately spreading misinformation
- submitting fabricated evidence
- pressuring participants to disclose personal information
- promoting unsafe AI use after risks have been explained
- using project spaces for unsolicited sales or spam

---

# Protected Characteristics and Inclusion

Discrimination is not accepted on the basis of:

- age
- disability
- gender reassignment
- marriage or civil partnership
- pregnancy or maternity
- race
- religion or belief
- sex
- sexual orientation
- nationality
- socioeconomic background
- caring responsibilities
- digital confidence
- professional status
- any other protected or personal characteristic

This list is not exhaustive.

---

# Accessibility

Participants should make reasonable efforts to keep contributions accessible.

This includes:

- using plain English
- avoiding unexplained jargon
- using descriptive links
- adding alt text where relevant
- using accessible document structures
- avoiding colour-only meaning
- being patient with different communication styles

Accessibility concerns should be treated as quality issues, not optional preferences.

---

# Privacy

Do not share:

- personal data without permission
- safeguarding case information
- identifiable beneficiary stories
- employee records
- donor records
- health information
- private messages
- passwords
- API keys
- confidential documents
- privileged legal advice

Where examples are needed, use:

- fictional details
- synthetic data
- aggregated findings
- suitably anonymised summaries
- public information

---

# Safeguarding

If a contribution includes safeguarding information, stop before publishing it.

Do not use project spaces to:

- report an active safeguarding concern
- share case details
- seek urgent safeguarding advice
- name a person involved in a safeguarding issue

Use the appropriate safeguarding route within the relevant organisation or statutory service.

The project does not replace safeguarding procedures.

---

# Research Participation

Research participation must be:

- voluntary
- informed
- proportionate
- privacy-conscious
- respectful
- easy to withdraw from where applicable

Participants should not be pressured to share:

- sensitive personal information
- confidential organisational information
- beneficiary case details
- safeguarding incidents
- internal disputes

Research and marketing consent should remain separate.

---

# Constructive Disagreement

Disagreement is welcome when it improves the work.

Good disagreement:

- identifies the specific issue
- explains the evidence
- separates fact from opinion
- acknowledges uncertainty
- proposes an alternative
- remains respectful

Poor disagreement:

- attacks the person
- dismisses lived experience
- relies on status rather than evidence
- deliberately misrepresents another contribution
- repeats a claim after it has been disproved without new evidence

---

# Evidence and Misinformation

Contributors should:

- use current and relevant sources
- avoid fabricated citations
- avoid presenting marketing as independent evidence
- distinguish law from recommendation
- disclose uncertainty
- correct errors when identified

Deliberate misinformation may result in contribution restrictions.

---

# Commercial Conduct

Project spaces should not be used for:

- unsolicited sales
- affiliate promotion without disclosure
- vendor advertising disguised as guidance
- pressure to purchase a tool or service
- manipulating tool ratings
- suppressing valid criticism

Relevant commercial relationships must be disclosed.

---

# Vendor Participation

AI providers and vendors may contribute factual corrections and official documentation.

They must not:

- control editorial conclusions
- remove supported criticism
- influence safety ratings through commercial pressure
- use contributor spaces as advertising
- conceal conflicts of interest

---

# Enforcement Responsibilities

Matthew James Nel is the original creator and current primary maintainer.

The maintainer may:

- request changes
- edit or remove harmful content
- close issues
- reject pull requests
- restrict participation
- block accounts
- refer serious matters to the appropriate platform or authority

Enforcement should be proportionate and documented where appropriate.

---

# Enforcement Guidelines

## 1. Correction

For minor or accidental issues:

- explain the concern
- request a change
- provide guidance
- allow correction

## 2. Warning

For repeated or more serious issues:

- issue a clear warning
- describe expected behaviour
- document the concern

## 3. Temporary Restriction

For continued or significant misconduct:

- temporarily restrict participation
- close or lock discussions
- pause review of contributions

## 4. Permanent Restriction

For serious harm or repeated misconduct:

- block participation
- remove access where possible
- document the decision privately or publicly as appropriate

## 5. Escalation

Where conduct involves:

- credible threats
- unlawful content
- safeguarding risk
- serious privacy breach
- stalking
- doxxing

The matter may be escalated to:

- GitHub
- the relevant organisation
- safeguarding leads
- platform moderators
- legal authorities
- emergency services

---

# Reporting a Concern

Reports should include:

- where the issue occurred
- what happened
- relevant links or screenshots
- whether anyone may be at immediate risk
- the preferred contact method

Do not include unnecessary personal data.

A dedicated reporting route should be added before public launch.

Until then, contact Matthew James Nel through:

https://www.linkedin.com/in/matthewjamesnel

Do not use LinkedIn for urgent safeguarding or emergency concerns.

---

# Confidentiality

Reports will be handled as privately as possible.

Information may be shared only where necessary to:

- investigate
- protect participants
- comply with law
- manage safeguarding
- enforce platform rules

Confidentiality cannot be guaranteed where there is a serious risk of harm or a legal duty to disclose.

---

# Appeals

A person affected by an enforcement decision may request review.

The review should consider:

- the original conduct
- available evidence
- severity
- pattern of behaviour
- impact on others
- whether the response was proportionate

Where the project gains additional maintainers, appeals should be reviewed by someone not responsible for the original decision where possible.

---

# Good-Faith Mistakes

The project recognises that people make mistakes.

Not every unclear comment or weak contribution is misconduct.

The preferred response to a good-faith mistake is:

- explanation
- correction
- learning
- improvement

The Code of Conduct should not be used to suppress respectful disagreement.

# Success Criteria

This Code of Conduct succeeds when:

- people can contribute without fear of harassment
- non-technical contributors feel welcome
- lived experience is treated respectfully
- privacy and safeguarding are protected
- disagreements remain constructive
- commercial influence remains transparent
- enforcement is proportionate
- the project remains open without becoming unsafe

The goal is a community that is useful, kind, evidence-led and accountable.
