# Purpose

This document is the central evidence base that supports the entire knowledge base.

It records why the project exists, what evidence supports it, where uncertainty remains and which sources should be monitored over time.

This document should be updated regularly as new research, guidance and survey findings become available.

# Research Objectives

The evidence base should answer:

- Is there demand for AI guidance in UK charities?
- What risks concern charities most?
- What do regulators expect?
- Where are the largest knowledge gaps?
- What guidance already exists?
- What remains missing?
- How should the knowledge base evolve?

# Executive Summary

Current evidence indicates a clear opportunity for a practical, plain-English AI safety resource for UK charities.

Key themes include:

- increasing AI adoption
- low confidence
- informal AI use
- limited governance
- GDPR uncertainty
- safeguarding concerns
- demand for practical templates
- limited internal expertise

The strongest opportunity is a practical, workflow-led resource aimed primarily at small and medium-sized charities.

# Key Evidence Themes

## AI adoption

Evidence indicates growing AI use across UK charities, often without formal governance.

Monitor:

- Charity Digital Skills Report
- NCVO
- Charity Digital
- independent sector surveys

## Governance gap

Evidence consistently suggests:

- AI use is ahead of policy
- many organisations lack AI guidance
- trustees want practical governance
- staff need operational guidance
