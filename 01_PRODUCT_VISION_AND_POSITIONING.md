# Vision

Build the UK's most trusted, evidence-led knowledge base that helps
charities use artificial intelligence safely, confidently and
responsibly.

This project exists to bridge the gap between complex regulatory
guidance and everyday charity work.

Rather than teaching people how to become AI experts, this knowledge
base helps them make better decisions about using AI in real situations.

It should become the first place a charity visits when asking:

-   Can we use AI for this?
-   Is this safe?
-   Does this involve personal data?
-   What does the ICO say?
-   What should our policy include?
-   Which AI tool is appropriate?
-   What should never be entered into an AI tool?

# Mission

Help charities save time with AI without risking:

-   people's personal data
-   safeguarding information
-   confidentiality
-   organisational trust
-   legal responsibilities
-   service quality
-   public confidence

The project should make responsible AI adoption easier rather than more
intimidating.

# Product Promise

> Use AI to save time without risking people's data, trust or safety.

Every page, template, workflow and decision tool should reinforce this
promise.

# The Problem

Artificial intelligence is being adopted across the charity sector
faster than governance, confidence and guidance.

Many charities are already using tools such as ChatGPT, Claude,
Microsoft Copilot and Google Gemini informally.

However:

-   many organisations have no AI policy
-   staff are unsure what can safely be entered into AI tools
-   GDPR guidance often feels technical
-   safeguarding concerns are poorly understood
-   trustees want reassurance without reading hundreds of pages of
    regulation
-   small charities rarely have specialist legal or digital teams

There is a gap between regulatory guidance and practical day-to-day
decision making.

This knowledge base exists to close that gap.

# The Opportunity

Most existing resources are one of three things:

-   regulator guidance
-   governance documents
-   AI training courses

Very few provide:

-   plain English
-   charity-specific examples
-   practical workflows
-   editable templates
-   decision trees
-   operational guidance
-   transparent evidence
-   ongoing monthly updates

This project combines all of these.

Every recommendation should be:

-   evidence-led
-   proportionate
-   practical
-   transparent

-   charity-focused
-   understandable by non-technical readers

# Editorial Philosophy

Always answer the practical question first.

People arrive with tasks, not curiosity.

Instead of explaining AI first, explain what someone should do next.

Examples:

-   Can I upload meeting notes?
-   Can AI help write fundraising emails?
-   Can volunteers use ChatGPT?
-   What should never be pasted into AI?

The knowledge base should help people complete work safely.

# Signature Framework

The defining feature of the project is the **Before You Paste**
framework.

Before entering information into an AI tool, users should pause and ask
structured questions about:

-   personal data
-   confidentiality
-   safeguarding
-   organisational risk
-   AI tool approval
-   human review

This framework should become the recognisable identity of the project.

# Success Measures

The project succeeds when:

-   charities understand AI risks more clearly
-   staff make safer decisions
-   trustees feel more confident discussing AI
-   organisations create proportionate AI policies
-   unsafe AI practices reduce
-   guidance remains evidence-based and current
-   charities actively contribute feedback
-   the knowledge base becomes a trusted community resource

# Definition of Success

The knowledge base should feel like an experienced colleague sitting
beside someone and helping them make a sensible decision.

It should never feel like a legal textbook, an AI marketing website or a
static policy archive.
