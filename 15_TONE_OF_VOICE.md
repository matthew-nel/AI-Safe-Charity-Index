# Purpose

This document defines how every sentence in the knowledge base should sound.

The goal is not to sound like AI.

The goal is to sound like an experienced digital professional helping a charity make good decisions.

# Voice

The voice should be:

- calm
- practical
- warm
- direct
- evidence-led
- conversational
- confident without sounding certain about everything
- respectful of the reader's knowledge

# Write Like This

Use:

- "Start here."
- "Pause and check."
- "You can usually..."
- "The main risk is..."
- "If you're unsure, ask."
- "For most small charities..."
- "Here's a sensible first step."

# Avoid

Do not use:

- leverage
- utilise
- transformative
- best-in-class
- unlock
- revolutionary
- cutting-edge
- paradigm
- robust framework
- in today's rapidly evolving landscape

# Reading Level

Aim for:

- Plain English
- Short sentences
- One idea per paragraph
- Everyday vocabulary
- Minimal jargon
- Explain technical terms immediately

# Charity-first Language

Prefer:

- charity
- supporter
- volunteer
- trustee
- beneficiary
- service user
- community
- colleague

Avoid generic corporate language where a charity-specific term is clearer.

# Handling Uncertainty

When evidence is uncertain, say so.

Examples:

- "Current guidance suggests..."
- "The evidence is still developing."
- "This is likely to change."
- "There is not yet clear UK guidance on..."

Never pretend certainty where none exists.

# Explaining Risk

Do not exaggerate.

Instead:

- explain the risk
- explain why it matters
- explain what to do next

# Examples

Good:

"You can usually use AI to improve public website copy. Before you paste anything, check that it doesn't contain personal or confidential information."

Poor:

"Artificial intelligence represents a transformational opportunity for organisations seeking to optimise digital communication strategies."

# Human Review Test

Before publishing ask:

- Would I naturally say this to a charity manager?
- Could someone understand this without AI knowledge?
- Does it sound helpful rather than impressive?
- Does it avoid generic AI phrasing?

If the answer is no, rewrite it.

# Definition of Success

Readers should feel that the guidance was written by a knowledgeable person who understands charities, not by a machine.
