# Why This Exists

Artificial intelligence is moving faster than most charities can realistically keep up with.

Large organisations often have legal teams, data protection specialists, procurement departments and technical experts to help them adopt new technology safely.

Most charities do not.

Many small and medium-sized charities are asking the same questions:

- Can we use AI safely?
- Can we use ChatGPT at work?
- What can we upload?
- What does GDPR actually mean for AI?
- Do we need a policy?
- What should trustees know?
- Which tools can we trust?
- Where do we even start?

Finding clear answers is difficult.

Information is scattered across government guidance, regulator publications, technology companies, blogs, webinars and social media. Some advice is excellent. Some is outdated. Some is contradictory.

The result is uncertainty.

Some charities avoid AI completely because they fear getting it wrong.

Others adopt it without understanding the risks.

Neither approach helps the people charities exist to support.

---

# The Problem

Most guidance is written for one of two audiences:

- technical professionals
- large organisations with dedicated governance teams

Small and medium-sized charities are often left trying to translate complex advice into everyday decisions.

They need guidance that is:

- practical
- proportionate
- evidence-led
- written in plain English
- designed for real charity work

---

# Our Goal

> **Help charities save time with AI without risking people's data, trust or safety.**

Everything in this knowledge base supports that goal.

---

# What Makes This Different?

This project is not:

- an AI course
- a prompt library
- legal advice
- a product comparison website
- a technology blog

Instead, it is a practical knowledge base built around the everyday questions charities ask.

Every page aims to answer:

- What should I do?
- Why does it matter?
- What are the risks?
- What is the safer option?
- Where can I learn more?

---

# Built for the Charity Sector

The primary audience is:

- small charities
- medium-sized charities
- volunteer-led organisations
- community organisations

Larger charities will also find value, with dedicated guidance for more complex governance, procurement and assurance.

---

# Built in Public

This project is designed to improve over time.

Guidance is informed by:

- official publications
- academic research
- charity-sector experience
- community feedback
- user research
- real-world questions from charities

When guidance changes, the change should be visible through version history and release notes.

---

# Independent and Evidence-Led

Recommendations are based on evidence rather than marketing.

Where evidence is uncertain, the project will say so.

Where guidance changes, earlier recommendations will be updated rather than quietly replaced.

The aim is to earn trust through transparency.

---

# Community Research

This project actively invites charities to help shape future guidance.

Research may explore:

- AI tools being used
- confidence levels
- governance challenges
- barriers to adoption
- training needs
- policy gaps
- accessibility concerns
- emerging risks

Feedback directly informs future releases.

---

# Human Judgement Comes First

AI can help charities:

- draft
- summarise
- explain
- organise
- analyse

It should not replace:

- professional judgement
- safeguarding decisions
- legal advice
- trustee oversight
- accountability

The most important decision-maker should always be a person.

---

# Long-Term Vision

The ambition is to build the UK's most trusted independent knowledge base for responsible AI use in the charity sector.

A place where charities can:

- find trusted guidance
- understand complex topics
- learn at their own pace
- share experience
- contribute improvements
- adopt AI with confidence
