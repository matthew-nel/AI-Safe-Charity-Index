# Start Here

AI can help charities draft, summarise, plan and learn. It can also create privacy, safeguarding, accuracy and accountability risks when used without clear checks.

## Three Rules

1. Do not paste personal, confidential or safeguarding information into an unapproved AI tool.
2. Use the least information needed for the task.
3. Keep a human responsible for the output and the final decision.

## Ten-Minute Route

1. Read [Before You Paste](../before-you-paste/index.md).
2. Check whether the exact tool, account, feature and data type are approved.
3. Pick the relevant [task guide](../everyday-charity-work/index.md).
4. Escalate Amber or Red uses before continuing.
5. Record any new or changed AI use where your charity requires it.
6. Know the [AI incident route](../governance/incidents.md).

## Routes by Role

- **Staff and volunteers:** Before You Paste, the relevant task page, and AI incidents.
- **Managers and tool owners:** tool approval, DPIA triage, monitoring and human review.
- **Trustees and leaders:** governance, trustee assurance, safeguarding, automated decisions and incidents.
- **Data-protection leads:** AI and Data Protection, DPIA triage, individual rights and supplier checks.
- **Safeguarding leads:** beneficiary services, meetings, incidents and human escalation.
- **Digital or IT leads:** account plans, agents and connectors, cyber controls and tool decommissioning.

!!! note "Approved tool does not mean approved use"
    An AI service is approved only for the accounts, features, purposes and information recorded in your charity's approval. A new connector, agent, model, meeting feature or data source may need a fresh check.
