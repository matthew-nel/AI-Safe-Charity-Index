# Before You Paste

Before you paste information into an AI tool, stop and check what the information contains, where it may go, what the tool may do with it, and what could happen if the output is wrong.

This framework does not replace professional advice, a Data Protection Impact Assessment, safeguarding procedures, supplier due diligence or formal organisational approval.

## The Quick Check

Ask these questions in order:

1. What task am I trying to do?
2. What information am I planning to enter?
3. Could a person be identified directly or indirectly?
4. Does it include personal data?
5. Does it include higher-risk information?
6. Is the information confidential?
7. Has it genuinely been anonymised?
8. Is the AI tool approved for this use?
9. Is using this information necessary?
10. Could the output affect someone?
11. Could an inaccurate or biased output cause harm?
12. Who must review or approve the use?

## Green: Go Ahead With Normal Checks

Green uses are usually low risk. The information is public, fictional, synthetic or genuinely non-personal. The tool is approved for the task, no significant decision about a person is being made, and a person will review the output.

Examples:

- brainstorming campaign names
- rewriting already-public website copy
- summarising a public report
- creating a meeting agenda
- generating fictional examples

Green does not mean the output is automatically accurate.

## Amber: Pause and Check

Amber uses need more thought before you continue.

Pause when the task involves:

- personal data
- internal information
- unpublished documents
- donor, staff, volunteer or beneficiary information
- decisions that could affect people
- unclear tool settings or account terms

Check your charity's policy, speak to the right internal lead, remove unnecessary information, and use an approved route where one exists.

## Red: Stop

Stop when the information includes:

- safeguarding case details
- passwords, API keys or security credentials
- special category data without an approved arrangement
- confidential legal, HR or disciplinary material
- identifiable beneficiary case notes
- information for an automated decision that could significantly affect someone

Use your charity's established safeguarding, data-protection, legal, HR or senior-management process instead.

## Safer Alternatives

When in doubt:

- use a fictional example
- remove identifiers
- summarise the issue at a higher level
- use public information
- ask for structure or wording help without sharing the real case
- use an approved internal tool
- ask a manager, safeguarding lead or data-protection lead
