# AI in Fundraising

AI can help fundraisers draft, plan, test and summarise. It can also create risks around misleading content, donor privacy, vulnerability, profiling and trust.

The Fundraising Regulator says charitable fundraising institutions remain responsible and accountable for AI use in fundraising, including AI-generated outputs they use.

## Green Uses

Usually suitable with normal review:

- brainstorming public campaign ideas
- drafting generic social posts
- improving the clarity of public copy
- turning published impact information into a first-draft checklist
- creating internal planning structures without donor data

## Amber Uses

Pause and check before using AI for:

- donor segmentation
- fundraising emails based on supporter data
- grant applications using internal evidence
- AI-generated images or stories for public campaigns
- analysis of survey comments
- personalised donor communications
- fundraising content about vulnerable people

## Red Uses

Do not use a general-purpose or unapproved AI tool for:

- identifiable donor databases
- special category data
- fabricated testimonials or impact evidence
- synthetic beneficiary stories presented as real
- decisions about who should receive support, grants or contact without safeguards
- fundraising approaches that could exploit vulnerability

## Fundraising AI Checklist

Before using AI:

- confirm the tool and account are approved
- assess risk before the use starts
- check whether donor or supporter data is involved
- apply data minimisation
- review for accuracy and fairness
- check Code of Fundraising Practice expectations
- decide whether transparency about AI use is needed
- keep a record of important checks
- involve trustees for strategic or high-risk use
