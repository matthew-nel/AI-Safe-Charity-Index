# AI Meeting Notes and Transcription

AI meeting notes can save time, but recording and transcription can capture personal data, confidential discussions, special category data and third-party voices.

Do not treat transcription as a default admin feature.

## Before Recording or Transcribing

Check:

- the purpose of the recording or transcript
- who will be recorded
- whether personal or sensitive topics may arise
- whether everyone has been given clear notice
- the lawful and fair basis for processing
- who can access the transcript and summary
- how long the recording and transcript will be kept
- whether the tool/account is approved
- whether the meeting is too sensitive for AI notes

## Usually Lower Risk

- public webinar planning
- routine project meetings with no personal data
- agenda and action drafting after a human checks the result

## Higher Risk

- HR meetings
- complaints
- safeguarding discussions
- beneficiary case meetings
- trustee disputes
- legal or contract negotiations
- meetings involving children or vulnerable people

## Controls

- provide advance notice
- restrict access to recordings and transcripts
- avoid sensitive meetings unless specifically approved
- set retention periods
- check summaries for errors
- delete recordings when no longer needed
- record the decision for high-risk meetings

## Ask for Help When

- participants may feel unable to object
- special category or safeguarding information may arise
- the tool stores audio or transcript data outside your control
- the transcript could affect an employment, safeguarding or service decision
