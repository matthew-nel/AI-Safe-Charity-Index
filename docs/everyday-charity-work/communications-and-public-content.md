# Communications and Public Content

AI can help draft, reformat and test public content, but a charity remains responsible for accuracy, dignity, accessibility and trust.

<div class="risk-card risk-green" markdown="1">

## Green Uses

- brainstorming campaign concepts from public information
- turning approved source text into format variants
- suggesting headings, alt-text drafts or plain-language alternatives
- creating clearly fictional early-stage visual concepts

</div>

<div class="risk-card risk-amber" markdown="1">

## Amber Uses

- public content about a real service, community or lived experience
- synthetic images of people or places
- fundraising stories that could be mistaken for real examples
- translation, captions or accessibility summaries for public use
- content using partner, funder or licensed assets

</div>

<div class="risk-card risk-red" markdown="1">

## Red Uses

- fabricated facts, quotations, case studies or research findings
- synthetic beneficiary stories presented as real
- AI-generated evidence about a real incident
- impersonation of a real person
- public legal, medical, safeguarding or crisis advice without qualified review

</div>

## Minimum Publication Check

Before publishing AI-assisted content:

- verify every factual claim against the original source
- confirm permission for real stories, images and quotes
- check for stereotypes, bias and harmful framing
- decide whether synthetic content needs disclosure
- check accessibility in the final format
- name the human editor responsible for the final version
