# Recruitment and HR

AI use in recruitment and HR can affect people's work, income, dignity and rights. Treat scoring, ranking, monitoring and disciplinary uses as high risk.

<div class="risk-card risk-green" markdown="1">

## Green Uses

- drafting a generic job description from approved role information
- creating interview-question ideas for human review
- producing a training outline
- rewriting public HR guidance in plain English

</div>

<div class="risk-card risk-amber" markdown="1">

## Amber Uses

- anonymised aggregate staff-survey summaries
- drafting recruitment materials
- summarising HR policies for a qualified person to check
- translating staff guidance

</div>

<div class="risk-card risk-red" markdown="1">

## Red Uses

- sourcing, filtering, ranking or rejecting candidates without a governed process
- analysing video, voice, face, emotion, personality or culture fit
- performance monitoring or disciplinary findings
- redundancy, pay, promotion or dismissal decisions
- inferring health, disability, union membership or other sensitive characteristics

</div>

## Controls

- complete DPIA screening early
- tell candidates or workers clearly what is used
- test job relevance, fairness and equality impact
- offer adjustments and a non-AI route where needed
- define meaningful human decision and challenge
- monitor outcomes and stop if harm appears
