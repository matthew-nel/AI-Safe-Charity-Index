# Beneficiary Services and Chatbots

AI that interacts with beneficiaries is not comparable to AI that drafts internal copy. The consequences of wrong, harmful or inaccessible output can be serious.

## Four Levels of Use

| Level | Example | Default risk |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Navigation | Points to opening times or human contact routes | Amber |
| Information support | Summarises approved public information | Amber |
| Personalised recommendation | Uses a person's circumstances to suggest options | Red until reviewed |
| Decision or professional advice | Determines eligibility, risk, safeguarding or crisis action | Red |

## Mandatory Controls

Any public bot or advice tool should:

- say clearly that it is AI
- never imply a human is responding
- show urgent-help and human-contact routes
- use approved, maintained knowledge sources
- avoid unnecessary personal data collection
- have retention information
- be tested for harmful advice and hallucination
- hand off safeguarding disclosures to a trained route
- be monitored for complaints and unequal outcomes
- have a kill switch

## Red Lines

Do not use a general-purpose AI tool as:

- a counsellor
- a crisis service
- a safeguarding lead
- a medical professional
- a legal adviser
- a gatekeeper for support
