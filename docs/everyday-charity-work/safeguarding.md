# Safeguarding and AI

AI must sit inside the charity's safeguarding system, not alongside it.

## Red by Default

Treat these as Red for general-purpose or unapproved AI tools:

- live safeguarding disclosures
- case records
- allegations or risk assessments
- identifiable information about children or adults at risk
- crisis, self-harm, abuse or exploitation advice
- automated triage or downgrade of concerns

## Minimum Rules

- do not present AI as a counsellor, crisis worker or safeguarding lead
- do not allow AI to close or downgrade a concern
- make emergency and human-contact routes prominent
- preserve records and handoffs where a disclosure occurs
- test harmful advice, dependency, self-harm, abuse and evasion scenarios
- let staff override or stop the service
- involve safeguarding specialists before launch

## Ask for Specialist Review When

- the AI use affects a beneficiary-facing service
- children or adults at risk may use the system
- special category or criminal-offence data may be processed
- a tool may make or influence a risk judgement
- a transcript or summary may contain safeguarding information
