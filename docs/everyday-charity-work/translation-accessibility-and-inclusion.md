# Translation, Accessibility and Inclusion

AI translation and accessibility tools can widen participation, but fluent output can still be wrong.

## Rules

- state the language, audience and specialist terms
- use a qualified or community reviewer for safety-critical content
- treat automated captions as drafts
- preserve speaker identity and non-speech information where needed
- offer another format or human interpreter where appropriate
- test with assistive technology and real users where possible
- do not infer disability or language need from unrelated behaviour
- review synthetic voice for intelligibility, consent and impersonation risk

## Red Uses

- unreviewed translation of safeguarding, legal, medical or rights information
- automated captions as the only accessible format for important content
- synthetic voice impersonating a real person
- AI summaries that remove essential warnings or eligibility criteria
