# Research Gaps and Priorities

The evidence base for charity AI use is growing quickly, but important questions remain unanswered. This page turns the July 2026 research packs into a practical agenda for researchers, funders and collaborators.

## Priority Gaps

| Gap | Why it matters | Suggested method | Priority |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Charity-size-specific adoption and governance | Micro, small, medium and large charities need different controls and support. | Stratified sector survey plus interviews by income band and activity type. | High, 6-12 months |
| Beneficiaries' views, especially children and adults at risk | AI governance must reflect the people affected by charity services, not only staff views. | Participatory research, focus groups and interviews with strong ethical safeguards. | High, 6-12 months |
| Charity-specific AI incidents and near misses | Charities need real lessons about data leaks, unsafe advice, bias, profiling and reputational harm. | Anonymous reporting pilot and case-study collection. | High, 6-12 months |
| Independent testing of workplace AI controls and connectors | Vendor documentation is necessary but not independent evidence of safety. | Controlled charity-environment testing of permissions, retention, connectors and oversharing. | High, 12 months |
| Volunteer and shadow AI use | Informal use may be where the most sensitive mistakes happen. | Anonymous surveys and focus groups with staff and volunteers. | High, 6-12 months |
| Effectiveness of governance interventions | Policies and registers should be tested for whether they actually reduce risk. | Action research with pre/post measures in partner charities. | High, 12 months |
| Long-term organisational effects | AI may change accountability, staff judgement and organisational culture over time. | Longitudinal case studies. | Medium, 12+ months |
| Public trust and synthetic media | AI-generated imagery and composite stories can affect trust and fundraising ethics. | Public surveys and controlled campaign testing with ethical safeguards. | Medium, 12 months |
| Accessibility and digital poverty | AI can assist inclusion or deepen exclusion. | Research with people facing digital poverty, accessibility barriers or language barriers. | Medium, 12+ months |
| Environmental impact | AI use has sustainability implications that are rarely addressed in charity governance. | Scoping study and practical reporting guidance. | Low-medium, 12+ months |

## Six-Month Research Agenda

1. Size-specific adoption and governance survey.
2. Volunteer and shadow AI use study.
3. Beneficiary perception scoping study.
4. Anonymous incident and near-miss reporting pilot.
5. Public-trust synthesis focused on AI in charity decisions and synthetic media.

## Twelve-Month Research Agenda

1. Independent testing of workplace AI connectors and controls.
2. Longitudinal organisational impact case studies.
3. Synthetic media and fundraising trust experiments.
4. Accessibility and digital poverty study.
5. Governance intervention evaluation projects.
6. Environmental impact scoping.

## Invitation to Researchers

This project welcomes collaboration on charity AI policy, safeguarding, public trust, donor ethics, data protection, procurement and practical governance.
