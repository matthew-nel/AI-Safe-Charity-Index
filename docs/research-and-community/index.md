## Research Interests

The project welcomes research conversations on:

- AI policy in charities
- responsible AI governance for small and medium charities
- trustee oversight and assurance
- staff confidence, training and adoption barriers
- AI use in fundraising, communications, HR, governance and service delivery
- AI, safeguarding and vulnerable people
- AI, GDPR and data minimisation in charity workflows
- approved-tools processes and practical risk assessment

## Ways to Contribute

You can help by:

- suggesting sources
- reviewing draft guidance
- proposing practical examples
- sharing anonymised workflow insights
- testing pages for clarity and accessibility
- identifying missing topics
- contributing to research packs

Do not submit personal data, safeguarding case details, confidential charity material or private research responses through public project spaces.

## Becoming a Collaborator

If you want to contribute regularly, request a GitHub collaborator invite through the contact page.

Include:

- your GitHub username
- the contribution area you want to help with
- relevant charity, research, policy, safeguarding, GDPR, cyber-security, accessibility or UX experience
- whether you want to contribute sources, review content, improve templates, test the site or help with design

Suggested subject line:

> AI Safe Charity Index collaborator invite request

[Request Collaborator Access](../about/contact.md#request-collaborator-access){ .md-button .md-button--primary }

Do not include personal data, safeguarding case details, confidential charity information or private research responses in the first message.

Once approved, the project owner can invite you through GitHub. You will need to accept the GitHub invitation before you can collaborate directly on the project.
