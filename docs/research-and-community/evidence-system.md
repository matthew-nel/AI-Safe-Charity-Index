# Contributor and Evidence System

AI Safe Charity Index should stay useful because it is practical, but trustworthy because it is research-led. This page sets the contribution rules for research updates, source suggestions and page reviews.

## Evidence Levels

| Level | Evidence type | How to use it |
| --- | --- | --- |
| A | Legislation and statutory regulators | Highest authority for legal and regulatory duties. |
| B | Official government and public-body guidance | Strong guidance, especially where charities operate in comparable contexts. |
| C | Peer-reviewed academic research | Strong for harms, bias, evaluation and methods; may need translation into charity practice. |
| D | Established charity-sector research | Useful for adoption, practice and sector context; check methodology and sponsorship. |
| E | Official vendor documentation | Necessary for product features, DPAs, retention and training settings, but not independent proof of safety. |
| F | Credible professional analysis | Useful interpretation; verify against higher-level sources where possible. |
| G | Documented organisational examples | Useful examples; may not generalise across charity sizes or service types. |
| H | Reputable media reporting | Useful for incidents and public debate; verify with primary sources where possible. |
| I | Anecdotal or unverified claims | Do not use for substantive claims without corroboration. |

## Contribution Rules

- Separate facts, interpretation and recommendations.
- Do not add vendor marketing claims without independent evidence.
- Do not submit personal data, safeguarding case details or confidential charity material.
- Use direct source links, not search-result pages.
- Record source limitations, especially sponsorship, small sample size or non-UK context.
- Flag where legal, safeguarding, cyber-security, employment or data-protection review is needed.
- Keep public pages plain-English and usable by small charities.

## Source Submission Checklist

For each source, capture:

- organisation or author
- title
- date and last updated date
- jurisdiction
- source type and evidence level
- direct URL
- date accessed
- relevance to charity AI safety
- limitations or conflicts of interest
- pages or claims the source supports

## Page Review Checklist

Before a page is treated as stable, check:

- Are high-impact claims sourced?
- Are Green, Amber and Red recommendations proportionate?
- Is the page clear about when specialist advice is needed?
- Does it avoid giving legal, safeguarding or clinical advice?
- Does it explain uncertainty rather than hiding it?
- Are links current and direct?
- Is the language plain enough for busy staff and trustees?

## Correction and Challenge Process

If you think a page is wrong or incomplete:

1. Identify the page and section.
2. Explain the issue.
3. Provide evidence or a source link.
4. Suggest a correction.
5. Declare any relevant conflict of interest.

For regular contribution, request collaborator access through the [contact page](../about/contact.md#request-collaborator-access).
