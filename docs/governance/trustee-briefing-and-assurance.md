# Trustee Briefing and Assurance

Trustees remain responsible for charity decisions involving AI. AI can inform decisions, but it cannot carry trustee judgement or accountability.

## Board Questions

Ask:

- What AI tools are staff, volunteers and contractors using?
- Which uses involve personal, confidential, safeguarding or financial information?
- Which uses could affect access to services, employment, grants or support?
- Who approves tools, account plans, connectors and use cases?
- How are outputs checked by competent people?
- What incidents, near misses, complaints and overrides occurred?
- Which supplier, model, connector or terms changed?
- Are any approvals, DPIAs or reviews overdue?
- What decisions need trustee input?

## Trustee Dashboard

A simple assurance report can include:

- approved tools and use cases
- high-risk proposals under review
- new models, connectors or agents
- incidents and near misses
- training completion
- DPIA and supplier-review status
- user access and offboarding checks
- planned retirements or changes

## When Trustees Should Be Involved

Bring a use to trustees when it may materially affect:

- strategy, reputation or public trust
- safeguarding or vulnerable people
- major service delivery
- employment or volunteer rights
- fundraising ethics
- material contracts or spend
- serious incidents
