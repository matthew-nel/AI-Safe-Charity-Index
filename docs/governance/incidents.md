# AI Incidents

An AI incident is any AI-related event that may create harm, disclose information, mislead people, affect a decision unfairly or damage trust.

## Examples

- personal data pasted into an unapproved tool
- safeguarding details shared with a chatbot
- confidential files exposed through an AI connector
- fabricated information published as fact
- a harmful or biased output used in a decision
- AI-generated images presented as real evidence
- an agent sends, deletes or changes something without approval

## First Steps

1. Stop the use.
2. Preserve basic facts: tool, account, date, prompt, output and people affected.
3. Tell the right internal lead.
4. Do not paste more information into the tool to investigate.
5. Follow existing data-protection, safeguarding, security or complaints processes.
6. Do not hide the mistake, delete evidence or ask the AI tool to "clean up" the incident.

## Four Triage Lanes

Many AI incidents need more than one route.

| Lane | Examples | Lead |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Data protection | personal data pasted, transcript exposed, rights request affected | Data-protection lead |
| Safeguarding/service | harmful advice, missed escalation, child or adult at risk affected | Safeguarding or service lead |
| Cyber/agent | compromised account, prompt injection, connector leak, unauthorised action | IT/security lead |
| Content/reputation | fabricated quote, misleading image, wrong public claim | Communications or senior lead |

## Review Questions

- Was personal or confidential information disclosed?
- Could a child, beneficiary, staff member, volunteer or donor be harmed?
- Was a decision affected?
- Was the output published or shared externally?
- Can the data be deleted or access restricted?
- Does the charity need to notify anyone?
- What control failed?
- What should change before the tool is used again?

## Personal-Data Breach Assessment

Not every AI incident is a reportable personal-data breach. If a personal-data breach is likely to result in a risk to people's rights and freedoms, the charity should normally notify the ICO within 72 hours of becoming aware of it.

Record:

- when the charity became aware
- what data was involved
- how many people may be affected
- likely consequences
- containment steps
- whether the ICO was notified
- whether affected people were told
- reasons for the decision

## Other Routes to Consider

Depending on the facts, consider:

- safeguarding referral or internal safeguarding review
- Charity Commission serious-incident reporting
- insurer notification
- supplier or processor notice
- police or Action Fraud route
- payment recall or finance controls
- contract, HR or employment process
- public correction or takedown

## After Containment

- pause or narrow the affected approval
- revoke connectors or user access if needed
- reset credentials where relevant
- check logs and file permissions
- review training and policy wording
- update the AI-use register
- agree when the tool may be used again
