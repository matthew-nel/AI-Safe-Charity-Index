# AI Incidents

An AI incident is any AI-related event that may create harm, disclose information, mislead people, affect a decision unfairly or damage trust.

## Examples

- personal data pasted into an unapproved tool
- safeguarding details shared with a chatbot
- confidential files exposed through an AI connector
- fabricated information published as fact
- a harmful or biased output used in a decision
- AI-generated images presented as real evidence
- an agent sends, deletes or changes something without approval

## First Steps

1. Stop the use.
2. Preserve basic facts: tool, account, date, prompt, output and people affected.
3. Tell the right internal lead.
4. Do not paste more information into the tool to investigate.
5. Follow existing data-protection, safeguarding, security or complaints processes.

## Review Questions

- Was personal or confidential information disclosed?
- Could a child, beneficiary, staff member, volunteer or donor be harmed?
- Was a decision affected?
- Was the output published or shared externally?
- Can the data be deleted or access restricted?
- Does the charity need to notify anyone?
- What control failed?
- What should change before the tool is used again?
