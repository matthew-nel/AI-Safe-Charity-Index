# AI Policy and Risk Ownership

Proportionate does not mean informal or undocumented. A small charity can still have clear AI rules, a named owner and an incident route.

## Minimum Policy Contents

An AI policy should state:

- who owns AI governance
- which tools, accounts and features are approved
- what staff and volunteers must not enter
- when to use Green, Amber and Red routes
- when DPIA, safeguarding, HR, equality, procurement or security review is needed
- how human review works
- how new use cases are recorded
- how incidents and near misses are reported
- when trustee visibility is required

## Ownership Model

Assign named roles for:

| Role | Responsibility |
| --- | --- |
| AI owner | Maintains approved use list and review cycle |
| Tool owner | Owns a specific system, settings and users |
| Data-protection lead | Reviews personal-data risks, DPIAs and rights |
| Safeguarding lead | Reviews risks to children, adults at risk and service users |
| IT/security lead | Reviews accounts, connectors, access and incidents |
| Trustee lead | Provides board visibility and challenge |

## Escalation Triggers

Escalate when AI may affect:

- access to services, grants, employment or support
- children, adults at risk or vulnerable people
- special category or criminal-offence data
- safeguarding or casework
- payments, fraud checks or financial controls
- external publication or public trust
- connected systems or autonomous actions
