# Perplexity

Perplexity can help discover sources, compare public guidance and produce cited research drafts.

<div class="risk-card risk-green" markdown="1">

## Green Uses

- finding current public sources
- creating search maps and reading lists
- comparing public guidance before opening and checking each source

</div>

<div class="risk-card risk-amber" markdown="1">

## Amber Uses

- uploading internal documents in approved Enterprise use
- policy research where citations influence public guidance
- enterprise knowledge connectors
- research on sensitive organisational topics

</div>

<div class="risk-card risk-red" markdown="1">

## Red Uses

- treating generated citations as verified evidence
- uploading personal data to consumer plans
- copying long copyrighted passages
- publishing unverified legal or safeguarding conclusions

</div>
