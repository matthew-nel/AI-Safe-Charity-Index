# Lawful Basis and Transparency

Define the purpose before choosing the tool or lawful basis. "Using AI" is not a purpose.

A useful purpose sounds more like:

- drafting a first version of a public event description
- transcribing a meeting so an authorised employee can prepare agreed minutes
- summarising survey themes after direct identifiers have been removed
- helping a trained caseworker find public guidance faster

If personal data is involved, record the Article 6 lawful basis before processing starts. If special category or criminal-offence data is involved, identify the additional legal condition and safeguards.

## Consent Needs Real Choice

Consent is appropriate only where people can refuse or withdraw without detriment. Be especially cautious where the person is an employee, volunteer, service user, child, adult at risk or someone who depends on the charity's support.

For many charity settings, a clear explanation, a lawful basis other than consent, and a workable alternative may be more realistic than asking for consent that is not freely given.

## What to Tell People

People should be able to understand:

- that AI is being used where this materially affects the processing
- what personal data is used
- why it is used
- who receives it
- whether a supplier processes it
- whether it may be transferred internationally
- how long it is kept
- how human review works
- how to exercise rights or complain

## Small-Charity Check

Before the use starts, write down:

| Question | Minimum record |
| --- | --- |
| What is the purpose? | One plain-English sentence |
| What data is used? | Categories, not a vague label |
| What lawful basis applies? | Article 6 basis and reason |
| Is special category data involved? | Article 9 condition or "not involved" |
| How are people told? | Privacy notice, meeting notice, script or other route |
| What is the human role? | Named reviewer or role |
