# Individual Rights

AI use does not remove people's data-protection rights. In some cases, it makes rights harder to handle unless the charity keeps good records.

## Rights to Plan For

Depending on the use, people may need routes to:

- be told how their data is used
- access personal data held about them
- correct inaccurate data
- object to certain processing
- ask for restriction or erasure where the legal conditions apply
- challenge a significant automated decision
- make a complaint

## Records That Make Rights Work

Keep enough information to answer:

- which tool, account and feature was used
- what personal data was entered, uploaded, connected or generated
- whether the provider stores prompts, outputs, files, logs or recordings
- who can retrieve or delete the data
- whether outputs influenced a decision about a person
- what human review happened

## AI Output Can Be Personal Data

An AI-generated summary, score, profile, prediction or note about an identifiable person may itself be personal data. It may also be inaccurate, incomplete or unfair.

Do not copy AI output into a case record, HR file, donor record or safeguarding record unless a competent person has checked it and the record clearly fits the charity's purpose.

## When to Escalate

Escalate to the data-protection lead if:

- a person asks whether AI was used about them
- an AI output may be wrong in a record
- an AI system produced a score, risk rating or recommendation
- a supplier cannot support access, correction or deletion
- the use involves automated decision-making or profiling
