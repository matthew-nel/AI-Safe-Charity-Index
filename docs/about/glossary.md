# Glossary

## Agentic AI

AI that can plan steps and act through tools or connected systems, not only produce an answer.

## Anonymisation

Changing information so a person is no longer identifiable by anyone reasonably likely to access it. This is stronger than simply removing a name.

## Article 6 Lawful Basis

The UK GDPR basis that allows personal data to be processed for a specific purpose.

## Article 9 Condition

An additional condition needed for processing special category data, alongside an Article 6 lawful basis.

## Automated Decision

A decision produced by automated processing. Legal rules become especially important where there is no meaningful human involvement and the effect is significant.

## Connector

A link that lets an AI retrieve information from or act in another service, such as email, file storage, CRM or project-management software.

## Controller

The organisation that decides why and how personal data is processed.

## Criminal-Offence Data

Personal data relating to criminal convictions, offences, allegations or related security measures.

## Data Flow

A map of where data comes from, which tool or supplier receives it, where it is stored, who can access it and where it goes next.

## Data Minimisation

Using only the personal data genuinely needed for the purpose.

## Data Subject

The identified or identifiable person the personal data is about.

## DPIA

A Data Protection Impact Assessment. A structured assessment required before processing likely to create high risk to people.

## Fine-Tuning

Training a model further on selected data to change how it behaves for a specific purpose.

## Foundation Model

A general model trained on broad data that can be adapted or prompted for many tasks.

## Grounding

Giving an AI system specific sources or data to use when answering.

## Hallucination

An AI output that sounds plausible but is wrong, unsupported or invented.

## Human Oversight

Competent human involvement that can identify problems and change the result.

## Inference

Running data through a trained model to produce an output.

## International Transfer

Moving or making personal data accessible outside the UK in a way covered by transfer rules.

## Legal or Similarly Significant Effect

An effect that changes someone's legal rights or has similarly serious practical impact, such as access to work, support, credit, services or important opportunities.

## Model

The AI system that produces predictions, summaries, classifications, images, text or other outputs.

## Personal-Data Breach

A security incident that leads to accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, disclosure of, or access to personal data.

## Plugin or App

An added capability that lets an AI tool connect to another system, retrieve data or perform actions.

## Processor

An organisation that processes personal data on a controller's instructions.

## Profiling

Automated processing that evaluates or predicts aspects of a person, such as performance, interests, behaviour, risk, eligibility or preferences.

## Prompt Injection

Instructions hidden in content that try to make an AI system reveal information or act unexpectedly.

## Pseudonymisation

Replacing identifiers while keeping separate information that could reconnect the data to a person. Pseudonymised data can remain personal data.

## Red Teaming

Testing a system by deliberately trying to make it fail, reveal information or behave unsafely.

## Retention

How long information is kept before deletion, archiving or review.

## Solely Automated Decision

A decision made without meaningful human involvement.

## Special Category Data

Sensitive personal data such as health, ethnicity, religion, political opinions, trade-union membership, sex life, sexual orientation, genetic or biometric data used for identification.

## Subprocessor

A further supplier used by a processor to help process personal data.

## System Prompt

Instructions set by the tool or organisation that guide how the AI should behave.

## Synthetic Media

AI-generated or altered image, audio or video.

## Training

Using data to build or improve a model's behaviour.
