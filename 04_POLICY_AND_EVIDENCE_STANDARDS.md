# Purpose

This document defines how evidence is gathered, assessed, cited,
maintained and reviewed across the knowledge base.

The goal is to ensure every recommendation is trustworthy, transparent
and proportionate.

# Evidence Principles

The knowledge base must:

-   distinguish facts from recommendations
-   prioritise authoritative UK sources
-   acknowledge uncertainty
-   review content regularly
-   never invent regulatory requirements
-   update guidance when evidence changes

# Evidence Hierarchy

## Tier 1 -- Primary Sources

Use wherever possible.

-   Information Commissioner's Office (ICO)
-   UK Government
-   Charity Commission for England and Wales
-   Relevant legislation
-   Official documentation from AI providers

## Tier 2 -- Trusted Sector Bodies

-   NCVO
-   Charity Digital
-   CAST
-   DataKind UK
-   SCVO
-   Charity Excellence
-   Other recognised UK charity bodies

## Tier 3 -- Independent Research

-   Peer-reviewed papers
-   Universities
-   Independent research organisations
-   Professional reports

## Tier 4 -- Industry Commentary

Use only to support or interpret primary evidence.

Do not rely on blogs or opinion pieces where primary evidence exists.

# Facts vs Recommendations

Facts describe:

-   legal requirements
-   regulator guidance
-   documented research
-   provider statements

Recommendations explain:

-   practical ways to apply evidence
-   proportionate governance
-   suggested workflows
-   operational good practice

Always label recommendations clearly.

# Citation Rules

Every substantive page should include:

-   primary sources
-   review date
-   evidence level
-   version number

Where multiple sources support a claim, prefer UK-specific evidence.

# Conflicting Guidance

If trusted sources disagree:

1.  explain the difference
2.  identify the stronger evidence
3.  state any uncertainty
4.  recommend the lowest-risk practical approach

Never hide disagreement.

# Source Register

Maintain a source register including:

-   source ID
-   title
-   publisher
-   publication date
-   review date
-   URL
-   topic
-   evidence tier
-   notes

# Claim Register

Important claims should include:

-   claim ID
-   supporting sources
-   affected pages
-   confidence level
-   review status

# Confidence Levels

High: Multiple authoritative sources agree.

Medium: Evidence is good but evolving.

Low: Limited evidence or rapidly changing area.

# Monthly Review

Each month:

-   review new ICO guidance
-   review Charity Commission updates
-   review NCVO and sector guidance
-   review AI provider documentation
-   assess survey findings
-   identify affected pages
-   prepare release notes

# Legal Boundaries

This knowledge base provides practical guidance.

It is not legal advice.

Where appropriate, recommend seeking specialist advice for:

-   complex GDPR issues
-   safeguarding concerns
-   automated decision-making
-   procurement
-   enterprise governance

# Editorial Integrity

Do not exaggerate.

Do not speculate.

Do not present opinion as fact.

Where evidence is incomplete, say so.

# Definition of Success

Readers should understand:

-   what is known
-   what is recommended
-   what remains uncertain
-   where evidence comes from
-   when guidance was last reviewed
