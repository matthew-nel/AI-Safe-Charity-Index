# Purpose

This document defines the regulators, government sources, charity-sector bodies, standards organisations and AI providers that should be monitored for changes affecting the AI Safe Use Knowledge Base for UK Charities.

The watchlist helps the project:

- detect relevant legal and regulatory changes
- identify new charity-sector guidance
- track provider privacy and product changes
- review high-risk guidance before it becomes outdated
- plan monthly and quarterly updates
- prioritise urgent corrections
- maintain clear review responsibilities

# Core Principle

> Monitor the sources most likely to change the meaning, safety or accuracy of public guidance.

The watchlist is not a general news feed.

It should focus on changes that may affect:

- AI use
- data protection
- safeguarding
- trusteeship
- charity governance
- fundraising
- employment
- procurement
- accessibility
- cybersecurity
- provider privacy
- automated decision-making
- public trust

# Watch Priority Levels

## Critical

Review monthly and whenever a material alert appears.

Use for sources that can directly affect legal, regulatory or high-risk guidance.

## High

Review monthly or quarterly depending on update frequency.

Use for trusted charity-sector bodies and major provider documentation.

## Medium

Review quarterly.

Use for professional bodies, standards and specialist sector guidance.

## Monitor

Review every six to twelve months or when a relevant development occurs.

Use for broader research and lower-frequency sources.

## Information Commissioner's Office

**Priority:** Critical  
**Review frequency:** Monthly

# Monthly Watch Process

For each Critical and High source:

1. Check for updates.
2. Record the date checked.
3. Identify material changes.
4. Compare with the Source Register.
5. Identify claims affected.
6. Identify pages affected.
7. Flag urgent issues.
8. Set the next check date.
9. Record no change where appropriate.

# Quarterly Watch Process

Review all Critical sources, all High sources, selected Medium sources, provider tool pages, safeguarding guidance, accessibility standards, sector reports and relevant international developments.

Produce:

- sources checked
- material updates
- no-change confirmations
- claims affected
- pages affected
- review priorities
- recommended release changes

# Material Change Test

A change is material when it may affect:

- legal interpretation
- risk classification
- tool recommendation
- data-handling guidance
- safeguarding advice
- trustee responsibility
- template wording
- public trust
- user action

Do not treat cosmetic website changes as regulatory updates.

# Urgent Trigger Examples

Escalate immediately when:

- a regulator changes relevant guidance
- legislation changes
- a provider changes training or retention terms
- a major tool loses or gains an important privacy control
- a published recommendation becomes unsafe
- safeguarding guidance changes
- a serious public incident reveals a new risk
- a public page contains a material factual error

```text
Run the monthly Regulatory Watchlist review for the AI Safe Use Knowledge Base for UK Charities.

Produce:

- watchlist status updates

- Source Register updates
- Claim Register updates
- page impact report
- release recommendation

Do not treat provider marketing as independent evidence.
Do not treat international guidance as UK law.

```

# Watchlist Quality Checklist

Confirm:

- Was the correct source checked?
- Is the date recorded?
- Is the source current?
- Is jurisdiction clear?
- Is the change genuinely material?
- Are affected claims identified?
- Are affected pages identified?
- Is urgency appropriate?
- Is the next review date set?
- Is specialist review required?

# Definition of Success

The Regulatory Watchlist succeeds when the project:

- identifies important changes early
- avoids relying on outdated provider information
- keeps high-risk guidance current
- distinguishes UK requirements from international practice
- records both changes and no-change reviews
- supports monthly and quarterly releases
- gives users confidence that the resource is actively maintained

The watchlist should function as the early-warning system for the knowledge base.
