# Purpose

This document defines who the knowledge base is for, how content should
scale for different organisations and how users should navigate the
site.

The knowledge base is built primarily for **small and medium-sized UK
charities**. Larger and more complex charities are also supported
through dedicated pathways and advanced guidance.

# Audience Principles

Always write first for:

-   Non-technical readers
-   Busy charity professionals
-   Teams with limited AI experience
-   Organisations with limited digital capacity
-   People looking for practical answers

Do not assume readers have:

-   a legal team
-   a Data Protection Officer
-   an AI specialist
-   an information security team
-   enterprise software

# Charity Size Pathways

## Small charities (Primary)

Typical characteristics:

-   Volunteer-led or small staff teams
-   Limited budget
-   Informal AI use
-   No dedicated digital team
-   No AI policy

Priority guidance:

-   Before You Paste
-   First AI policy
-   Approved tools
-   Safe prompts
-   Staff awareness
-   Simple governance

## Medium-sized charities (Primary)

Typical characteristics:

-   Multiple teams
-   Regular AI use
-   More personal data
-   Formal management structure

Priority guidance:

-   Department guidance
-   Tool approval
-   AI use register
-   Risk assessment
-   Trustee oversight
-   Quarterly reviews

## Large and complex charities (Secondary)

Typical characteristics:

-   Multiple services
-   Dedicated legal, IT and data teams
-   Large-scale personal or special category data
-   Enterprise AI deployments

Priority guidance:

-   Enterprise governance
-   Procurement
-   DPIAs
-   Supplier assurance
-   AI inventories
-   Monitoring and audit

Advanced guidance should always appear behind clearly labelled sections
so it does not overwhelm smaller charities.

# Primary User Roles

Create learning paths for:

-   Trustees
-   CEOs and senior leaders
-   Charity managers
-   Communications teams
-   Fundraisers
-   Service delivery teams
-   HR
-   Volunteers
-   Digital and website teams
-   Data protection leads

Each role should have:

-   Recommended reading order
-   Typical AI use cases
-   Common risks
-   Suggested templates
-   Next steps

# Progressive Guidance

Every page should provide:

## Minimum viable approach

Suitable for small charities.

## Structured approach

Suitable for medium-sized charities.

## Advanced approach

Suitable for larger organisations.

Readers should never feel that enterprise governance is the minimum
expectation.

# Accessibility

Content must be:

-   Plain English
-   Mobile friendly
-   Easy to scan
-   Task based
-   Accessible to readers with low digital confidence

# Success Criteria

Users should quickly find guidance relevant to:

-   their organisation size
-   their role
-   the task they are trying to complete

The knowledge base should reduce uncertainty rather than increase it and
should help charities adopt AI safely at a pace that matches their
capacity.
