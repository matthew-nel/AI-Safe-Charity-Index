# Roadmap

This roadmap explains how the AI Safe Use Knowledge Base for UK Charities will develop over time.

The project is designed to grow in stages.

The immediate priority is to launch a useful, trustworthy minimum viable knowledge base for small and medium-sized charities.

The longer-term ambition is:

> **Become the UK's most trusted independent knowledge base for responsible AI use in the charity sector.**

---

# Roadmap Principles

The project should:

- solve real charity problems first
- publish only reviewed guidance
- prefer useful content over a large content volume
- keep small and medium-sized charities at the centre
- add advanced guidance without overwhelming the main audience
- improve through evidence and charity feedback
- remain maintainable by a small team
- avoid unnecessary technical complexity
- release iteratively
- show what has changed

---

# Project Stages

The roadmap is organised into eight stages:

1. Foundation
2. Minimum viable knowledge base
3. Technical build
4. Private review
5. Public beta

# Decision Rules

Prioritise work that:

- reduces risk
- answers repeated charity questions
- improves accessibility
- corrects inaccurate guidance
- supports small charities
- strengthens evidence
- removes friction
- improves maintainability

Defer work that:

- adds complexity without clear value
- serves vendors more than charities
- requires unmanageable maintenance
- duplicates existing trusted resources
- lacks evidence
- distracts from the core mission

# Known Risks

Current risks include:

- project scope becoming too broad
- too many foundation files before public content
- tool guidance becoming outdated
- overreliance on one AI provider
- limited maintainer capacity
- insufficient specialist review
- unclear licensing
- weak feedback participation
- accidental collection of sensitive data
- maintaining too many pages too early

Mitigation:

- launch a focused MVP
- review monthly
- publish known limitations
- automate low-risk validation
- seek specialist review
- prioritise high-value workflows
- defer non-essential features

---

# Definition of Roadmap Success

The roadmap succeeds when:

- the project launches with useful core guidance
- priorities remain visible
- progress can be reviewed honestly
- research influences development
- the resource grows without losing clarity
- small charities remain the primary audience
- governance scales with the project
- public trust grows through evidence and transparency

The roadmap should remain ambitious, practical and open to change.
